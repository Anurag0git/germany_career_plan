The E&TC Academic Pivot

If you decide to pursue an MS, you must navigate Germany's strict degree continuation rules.

❌ The Problem: The "ECTS Rule"

German public universities follow a "Consecutive Master's" model. You cannot drastically change your major. To enter a pure M.Sc. Computer Science, they scrutinize your Bachelor's transcript for specific ECTS credits:

  • ➖ 20+ credits in Theoretical CS (Automata)
  • ➖ 20+ credits in Practical CS (OS, Data Structures)
  • ➖ Pure Mathematics

Your E&TC degree focuses on Signals, Comm., and Microprocessors. Despite an 8.6 GPA, you will likely be rejected from pure CS programs due to missing theoretical CS credits.

✅ The Solution: Smart Program Selection

Do not apply for pure Computer Science. Leverage your E&TC background alongside your Cerence AI experience by applying to interdisciplinary programs:

M.Sc. Embedded Systems / IoT

Perfect fit. Combines your E&TC hardware base with your DevOps/Software skills.

M.Sc. Data Science / AI

Many accept E&TC grads, especially since you have professional AI industry experience.

M.Sc. Information Systems

Blends IT with management. Less strict on theoretical CS ECTS credits.
